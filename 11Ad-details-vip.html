Minimum age for selling pups in Ireland

Legally, puppies need to be at least 8 weeks old and completely weaned prior to when they can be sold and leave their mothers.


Puppies can be advertised for sale prior to the 8 weeks, however not sold . Nearer to 12 weeks is suggested for separating the puppie(s) from its mom.


This ad shows that the date of birth is May 17, 2023, by adding another 8 weeks to the D.O.B means the earliest legal date of sale is on Wednesday, July 12, 2023 .


This can be seen in the Animal Health and Welfare (Sale or Supply of Pet Animals) Regulations Act of 2019 Section 3 .

Safety tips for buyers
  • Ensure a happy and healthy pet purchase by taking necessary precautions.
  • Request ID from sellers and note their contact details; provide your own ID and proof of address.
  • Transfer the microchip into your name by sharing your details with the seller.
  • Before visiting, note microchip IDs from the advertisement and verify them with the certificates when viewing the puppies.
  • Avoid purchasing puppies without microchip certificates; verify that the microchips match the advertised information and are registered with approved databases like Animark, Fido, IKC, or ICC.
  • When meeting the seller, ensure the puppy's mom is present, check for health and socialization, and confirm the pup is at least 8 weeks old (preferably 10-12 weeks).
  • See the pups in their home environment, not in a parking area or other service location.
  • Inquire about the vet caring for the pup, and a sk to see vaccination certificates.
  • Avoid bringing young children to the first meeting to prevent potential issues or disappointments.
  • Steer clear of online transactions or upfront payments, as they may be scams, risking the loss of your deposit.
  • When considering a seller who offers delivery, take precautionary steps. Avoid upfront payments for delivery or holding the dog/puppy to prevent scams.
  • Verify microchip numbers and photos in the ad, ask for the seller's ID, check their phone's functionality, and note their vehicle registration.
  • Print the ad with dog/puppy photos and ensure it matches the advertised pet during the transaction.
